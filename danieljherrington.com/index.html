Career Overview

Let's commit to building something great.

Daniel Herrington is a 2011 Bagley College of electrical engineering graduate and recipient of the Hatcher Entrepreneurship Certificate at Mississippi State University. He is a patented inventor in smartphone technology, the current CSO of Walc, and the former CTO for Walc, SketchFactor, and College Rites.

After graduation, Daniel spent 2 years in Austin, Texas working for National Instruments as an applications engineer assisting in the company's record 34% growth pushing them beyond the $1 billion annual revenue mark. He advanced with National Instruments to Washington D.C. as a local field sales engineer and project manager to various government contractors. While at National Instruments, Daniel designed, supported, and sold large-scale automation and testing systems.

In June 2014, Daniel left National Instruments to co-found SketchFactor, a crowd-sourced pedestrian navigation company, as the CTO. The SketchFactor app launched on iOS and Android in August to much fanfare including a number 3 app store ranking behind Google Maps and Waze. Based in downtown Manhattan, SketchFactor pivoted to Walc in February of 2015 as a walking navigation utility.

After several years in the startup world, Daniel followed his love of product to Priceline.com in his first official role as a product manager. He strategized, pitched, planned, and released "Weeknder" with a team of designers and developers in his first six months at the company.

In his spare time, Daniel is an adventurer who travels frequently. He is an avid mountain biker and trail enthusiast having logged many hours on the Appalachian Trail and urban hikes across Europe. His love of live music captures most of his conversations that are not already engaged in automation controllers and his next Raspberry Pi project.
